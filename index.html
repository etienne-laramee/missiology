Responding to a pressing need

The creation of an Education Leadership Chair in Evangelical Protestant missiology aims to put missiology at the centre of theological studies. The Chair seeks to articulate through research and instruction a missional theology in Québec’s post Christian context by examining the relationship of faith and culture. The Chair constitutes a concrete response to current challenges faced by congregations in their efforts to bear witness in word and deed to Jesus and all his teachings to our contemporaries. It will be part of Université Laval’s Faculty of Theology and Religious Sciences and will reflect the university’s tradition of excellence in education and research and concretize its openness to other Christian traditions.

Missional theology is now at the heart of reflection and action of Christian churches worldwide. Missio Dei establishes the priority of the triune God’s being in action therefore characterizing God as a missionary God. In this case mission cannot be conceived of primarily or even essentially as an activity or program of the Church but must be rooted in God.

Missiology is the exegetical, theological and cultural study about the mission of God in the world and the ensuing task of the church. For that reason it is often defined as an inter-disciplinary field of reflection and action. When one shapes this discussion contextually and pursues this reflection in contexts of post-Christendom, one is seeking to relate culture, society mission. Missiology seeks a more adequate understanding of the apostolic mission of the Church while remaining faithful to the exegetical task of understanding the mind of the Biblical writers.
